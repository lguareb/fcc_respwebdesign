Hassan II, King of Morocco

Who is Hassan II?

Son of King Mohammed V, the restorer of Morocco's independence, Hassan II succeeded him on February 26, 1961. He promulgated a Constitution the following year but nevertheless ruled the country with an iron fist, without backing down. before the elimination of his adversaries or opponents like Mehdi Ben Barka and his own Minister of the Interior Mohamed Oufkir. At the end of a "green march", by which 350,000 Moroccans headed for the Spanish colony of Western Sahara, on November 6, 1975, Hassan II reunited it with the kingdom despite a contrary UN resolution.

When he died on July 23, 1999, his eldest son succeeded him under the name of Mohammed VI, becoming at the age of 35 the 23rd sovereign of the Alaouite dynasty and the 3rd to bear the title of King of Morocco. Prepared from childhood for his role as sovereign, he will have the difficult task of ensuring the modernization of the kingdom while preserving it from Islamist activities...

A King's Time line

  • July 9, 1929: Birth of Moulay El-Hassan, crown prince and eldest son of King Mohammed V of Morocco and his second wife Lalla Abla, in Rabat.
  • August 20, 1953: Beginning of the exile of the royal family after the dismissal of Mohammed V by France.
  • November 23, 1955: End of the exile of the royal family, which returns to Rabat.
  • April 7, 1956: With the end of the French and Spanish protectorates, Morocco is officially independent.
  • July 9, 1957: Official proclamation of King Mohammed V confirming the status of crown prince of his son, Moulay El-Hassan.
  • March 3, 1961: The crown prince ascends the Moroccan throne a few days after the death of his father under the name of Hassan II.
  • July 10, 1971: Attempted coup by army soldiers during the celebration of the king's birthday, a hundred guests lose their lives.
  • August 16, 1972: Second attack against Hassan II: his Minister of the Interior and Defense, Mohamed Oufkir, launches an air offensive against the sovereign's plane which was returning from France.
  • July 23, 1999: Death of Hassan II from a heart attack in Rabat.
